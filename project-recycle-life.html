Reframing organ donation through cultural insight and behavioral design

Challenge

Sweden faces a critical shortage of organ donors. While 83% of Swedes express a positive attitude toward organ donation, only 20% are registered donors. The issue wasn’t awareness, it was activation. Most existing campaigns relied on heavy, guilt-driven messaging. Impactful, yes. Effective, no, and often emotionally overwhelming. The core question became: “What if an everyday ritual could solve an overlooked public health challenge?”

Insight

Sweden is one of the world’s highest coffee consuming nations and among Europe’s strongest recyclers. Recycling is normalized, almost automatic.

“If we recycle almost everything — why shouldn’t we recycle life?”

Instead of presenting organ donation as a moral burden, we positioned it as a natural, loving continuation of life.

The Idea: “Återvinn din k(r)opp”

Launched around Valentine’s Day, the campaign connected organ donation to love and care. We designed custom coffee cups featuring elegant organ illustrations — symbolic rather than graphic.

The Unexpected Channel Choice

Train environments became a natural fit rather than an obvious starting point. Every day, SJ moves thousands of people throughout the country.

What do you actually do on a train?

You read a book, scroll on your phone, listen to music, or maybe drink a cup of coffee. Research shows that it is in these quiet moments people are more open to reflection and more open to actually making a decision.

At the same time, coffee consumption is high in this setting. The strength does not only lie in how many people are reached, it lies in the timing. By meeting people at the right moment, a simple everyday action like drinking coffee can carry a message and close the gap between intention and action. It shows how an unexpected channel like a coffee cup can reach and influence many people because it is already part of their daily routine.
