Recycle Life – Återvinn din k(r)opp

Reframing organ donation through cultural insight and behavioral design

Challenge

Sweden faces a critical shortage of organ donors. While 80% of Swedes express a positive attitude toward organ donation, only 20% are registered. The issue wasn’t awareness, it was activation. Most existing campaigns relied on heavy, guilt-driven messaging. Impactful, yes, but often emotionally overwhelming. The core question became: “How do we activate passive supporters without relying on shock?”

Insight

Sweden is one of the world’s highest coffee consuming nations and among Europe’s strongest recyclers. Recycling is normalized, almost automatic.

“If we recycle almost everything — why shouldn’t we recycle life?”

Instead of presenting organ donation as a moral burden, we positioned it as a natural, loving continuation of life.

The Idea: “Återvinn din k(r)opp”

Launched around Valentine’s Day, the campaign connected organ donation to love and care. We designed custom coffee cups featuring elegant organ illustrations — symbolic rather than graphic.

Channel Strategy

Train environments were selected strategically. Commuters experience idle, reflective moments — and coffee consumption is high in transit settings. The campaign transformed a habitual act (coffee drinking) into a frictionless activation opportunity.

Outcome

Recycle Life demonstrated how cultural insight and behavioral design can drive public health activation. The concept was strong enough to move from a classroom idea to a live campus implementation, proving the impact of behavioral design.
