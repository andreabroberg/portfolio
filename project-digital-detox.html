A data-driven CRM and loyalty concept designed to make Stadium's communication more relevant, useful, and connected to real customer needs.

Context

In this course, we talked a lot about CRM, such as newsletters and automated flows, and how they often do not feel very relevant to the customer.

In our case, we were tasked with developing a concept for how Stadium can increase loyalty among their club customers and get them to purchase more often.

Problem

The problem we focused on was that communication is often sent without being connected to the customer's actual needs or situation.

Many messages are generic and the same for everyone, which means they do not create much real value.

Solution

What we did was to design a data-driven loyalty program with automated flows based on customer behavior and seasonality.

Instead of sending general campaigns, we focused on communicating when a real need actually occurs, for example before the start of a season.

From Communication to Tool

For example, if the customer is a football team, the communication can highlight what the team usually buys before a season, what typically needs to be restocked after a few months, and what is likely to run out.

Instead of generic offers, this creates a clear overview of what is actually needed.

We also introduced features that make the solution more of a tool, not just communication. For example, a progress bar that shows how "season-ready" the team is based on what is missing. This way, Stadium becomes a support in planning, not just a place to shop.

Key Insight

What became clear to me is that the problem is rarely that companies communicate too much, but that the communication lacks relevance.

In this case, the focus was on using the data fully and turning it into something concrete and useful for the customer.
